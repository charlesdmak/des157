The war on drugs in the United States can be traced back to the 60’s where recreational drug use, which included substances like cocaine, marijuana, and LSD, was finding its way into the youth demographics. This resulting in part due to a rising rebellious sentiment created from social unrest. Legislation began in the 70s under President Nixon to focus on reducing drug abuse, declaring that drug abuse was “public enemy number one”. The next wave of legislation came under the Reagan era, in which a new smokable form of cocaine -- crack -- rose in popularity in areas like New York and Los Angeles. Consequently, levels of arrests and incarcerations continued to rise and prisons were continuing to fill with those who possessed and sold crack cocaine.
